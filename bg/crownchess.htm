Crown Chess

The Crownprincess succeeds the Queen






In Crown Chess a Crownprincess (also called Adjutant) replaces the queen. The Crownprincess slides in any direction, but on the orthogonals it slides only on one colour. The opposite colour squares are simply ignored. The value of the Crownprincess is at least 6, that is, at least the same as rook + pawn, or two light pieces. The Crownprincess is really an enhanced bishop that can also move orthogonally. Its main weakness is that it cannot give mate together with the king. In all other respects this variant is like standard chess.

The Crownprincess can only visit half the squares of the board. Despite this, it holds a value greater than a rook, which makes it a very interesting piece. The strategical tension becomes greater because the Crownprincess cannot simply be exchanged with a rook. At least two light pieces, or rook + pawn, is equivalent to a Crownprincess. This creates a game which is all but trivial. Due to its great value it cannot simply block enemy pieces. Note that the Crownprincesses cannot be exchanged with each other because they move on different square colours. Thus, early and shallow endgames cannot easily occur.

This is a piece which is easily graspable, and very agile. When it is exchanged, an unbalanced position, in terms of piece types, will result. It seems like the Crownprincess is a good substitute for the queen. Remember that the bishop moving on the same square colour as the enemy Crownprincess is more valuable than the other bishop. The king is not as vulnerable in Crown Chess as in Fide-chess. It is usually safe to remain in the centre with the king. The Crownprincess also appears in my Adjutant Chess.

crown princess (1) : the wife of a crown prince (2) : a female heir apparent or heir presumptive to a crown or throne (Webster's Dictionary)



The Crownprincess also slides on the orthogonals by ignoring the opposite colour squares. Note that it cannot leap over occupied squares of its own colour.
